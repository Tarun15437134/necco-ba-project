# Standard Operating Procedure
## Case Management Data Entry & Compliance Documentation

**SOP Number:** OPS-DATA-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** January 2024
**Owner:** Compliance & Operations
**Applies To:** All case managers and program staff — Ohio, Kentucky, West Virginia

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes consistent standards for entering, validating, and maintaining case data across all Necco programs and states. Consistent data entry ensures the accuracy of compliance reporting, supports quality improvement initiatives, and protects the organization during state audits.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all staff who enter, update, or review case records in the case management system, including case managers, supervisors, and program coordinators.

---

## 3. Required Fields — Case Record Creation

When opening a new case, the following fields must be completed before saving the record:

| Field | Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Case ID | Auto-generated | Do not alter |
| Worker ID | Required | Must match HR roster |
| Program Type | Required | Select from approved list only |
| State | Required | Must match worker's assigned region |
| Open Date | Required | Date of initial intake |
| Required Visit Date | Required | Calculated per program protocol (see Section 5) |
| Case Status | Required | Open, Closed, or Under Review |

**Incomplete records will be flagged by the system and escalated to the supervisor within 24 hours.**

---

## 4. Visit Documentation Standards

All visits must be documented within **24 hours** of the visit occurring. The following information is required:

- Actual visit date and time
- Visit type (in-home, office, virtual)
- Individuals present
- Safety assessment outcome
- Next visit date

If a visit cannot be completed as scheduled, staff must:
1. Document the reason in the case notes within 24 hours of the missed visit date
2. Notify their supervisor immediately
3. Reschedule within 48 hours

**Failure to document within 24 hours constitutes a compliance violation regardless of whether the visit occurred.**

---

## 5. Visit Frequency by Program Type

| Program Type | Required Visit Frequency | Documentation Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Family Preservation | Every 7–14 days | Within 24 hours |
| Foster Care | Every 30 days | Within 24 hours |
| Therapeutic Foster Care | Every 14–21 days | Within 24 hours |
| Residential | Every 30 days | Within 48 hours |

---

## 6. Incident Reporting Requirements

Any safety concern, near miss, or policy violation must be entered as an incident record within **4 hours** of the event or discovery.

Required incident fields:
- Incident type (select from approved list)
- Severity level (Low / Medium / High / Critical)
- Near-miss flag (Yes / No)
- Reported date and time
- Initial description (minimum 100 words)

**Critical and High severity incidents must be reported to the supervisor and compliance team immediately by phone, followed by system entry within 4 hours.**

---

## 7. Data Validation Rules

Supervisors are responsible for reviewing and approving all case records weekly. During review:

- Verify required fields are complete
- Confirm visit dates are accurate and match visit notes
- Flag any compliance variance greater than 3 days for review
- Escalate unresolved incidents past their resolution deadline

---

## 8. Non-Compliance with This SOP

Failure to follow this SOP may result in:
- Case record flagged as non-compliant in monthly reporting
- Escalation to supervisor and regional director
- Inclusion in quality improvement action plan
- Impact on performance review

---

## 9. Questions & Support

For questions about this SOP, contact the Compliance Team or your Regional Director. This SOP is reviewed annually and updated as needed based on state requirements and quality improvement findings.
